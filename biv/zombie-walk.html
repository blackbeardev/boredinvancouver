Vancouver Zombie Walk

Fancy going for a walk??

This one will scare you brainless!!

Events

Details

Looking for something a little different this weekend?? How about running around the town center with a bunch of strangers with blood dripping off your chin, moaning about brrraaaiiiinnnnssss!!!

The first Vancouver Zombie Walk was in 2005, when 400 participants terrified the city by marching into Pacific Center mall, then boarding a SkyTrain, before finally converging on Mountain View Cemetary. Since then, the event has only gotten more popular and gory.

Zombies are asked to be respectful to city and private property and not get all their blood and guts on cars, sidewalks, or random pedestrians to happen to get stuck in the middle of the hordes. Extra care is requested for children who may be scared by the spectacle, and organizers advise that if children seem disturbed, please move on and let their parents explain....

The first official zombie walk took place in Toronto in 2003, with a modest turnout of just six bodies. Since then, the zombie virus has spread rapidly, infecting cities throughout the world, from Singapore to Rio de Janeiro. Minneapolis-Saint Paul currently holds the Guinness record for the largest zombie walk, with approx. 8,030 corpses attending the 2012 event.
